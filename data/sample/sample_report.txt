SAMPLE 1 — Pneumonia + COPD + Diabetes (MEDIUM-HIGH RISK)
================================================================================

DISCHARGE SUMMARY

Patient Name: [REDACTED]
Date of Admission: 2026-05-01
Date of Discharge: 2026-05-07
Attending Physician: Dr. A. Ramesh, MD

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Acute shortness of breath and chest pain for 3 days.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
A 68-year-old male with known history of Type 2 diabetes mellitus, hypertension,
and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) presented to the emergency department
with progressive dyspnea and right-sided pleuritic chest pain. Patient also reported
productive cough with yellowish sputum for 5 days.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (HbA1c: 9.2%)
- Hypertension (poorly controlled)
- Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
- Chronic Kidney Disease Stage 3 (CKD)
- Previous hospitalization 2 months ago for COPD exacerbation

MEDICATIONS ON ADMISSION:
- Metformin 500 mg twice daily
- Amlodipine 5 mg once daily
- Aspirin 75 mg once daily
- Salbutamol inhaler PRN
- Lisinopril 10 mg once daily
- Simvastatin 40 mg at night

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:
- Temperature: 38.9°C (febrile)
- Blood Pressure: 158/96 mmHg
- SpO2: 88% on room air
- Respiratory Rate: 28/min
- Reduced air entry on the right lower zone with dullness on percussion

INVESTIGATIONS:
- WBC: 14.2 x10^9/L (elevated)
- CRP: 189 mg/L (significantly elevated)
- Creatinine: 2.1 mg/dL (elevated — consistent with CKD)
- HbA1c: 9.2% (poorly controlled diabetes)
- Blood Glucose: 18.4 mmol/L
- Chest X-Ray: Right-sided consolidation with pleural effusion noted

DIAGNOSIS:
1. Community-acquired pneumonia (right lower lobe)
2. Pleural effusion (right-sided)
3. Exacerbation of COPD
4. Poorly controlled Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
5. Hypertension
6. Chronic Kidney Disease Stage 3

TREATMENT:
- IV Amoxicillin-Clavulanate 1.2g every 8 hours (switched to oral at day 4)
- Clarithromycin 500 mg twice daily (added for atypical coverage)
- IV Hydrocortisone 100 mg every 8 hours (for COPD exacerbation)
- Salbutamol nebulization every 4 hours
- Insulin sliding scale for glycemic control
- Furosemide 40 mg IV (for pleural effusion / fluid management)
- Oxygen therapy — 2L via nasal prongs

HOSPITAL COURSE:
Patient responded to antibiotics by day 3 with defervescence and improving SpO2 (96% by day 5).
COPD exacerbation managed with nebulized bronchodilators and systemic corticosteroids.
Pleural effusion reduced significantly on repeat imaging at day 5.

DISCHARGE MEDICATIONS:
- Amoxicillin-Clavulanate 625 mg twice daily for 7 days
- Clarithromycin 500 mg twice daily for 5 more days
- Prednisolone 30 mg daily tapering over 1 week
- Metformin 500 mg twice daily (HELD — restart only after creatinine normalizes)
- Amlodipine 5 mg once daily
- Aspirin 75 mg once daily
- Simvastatin 40 mg at night
- Lisinopril 10 mg once daily

FOLLOW-UP:
- Review in 2 weeks with repeat chest X-ray
- Nephrology consultation for CKD management
- Diabetology review for HbA1c optimization
- Monitor serum potassium (on lisinopril + furosemide)

DISCHARGE CONDITION: Stable, improving

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:
- Congestive Heart Failure (EF 35%)
- Atrial Fibrillation (on anticoagulation)
- Hypertension
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

- Warfarin 5 mg once daily
- Digoxin 0.25 mg once daily
- Furosemide 40 mg once daily
- Carvedilol 12.5 mg twice daily
- Enalapril 10 mg once daily

- Amiodarone 200 mg once daily

- Troponin I: 0.08 ng/mL (mildly elevated)
- INR: 3.8 (supratherapeutic)

DIAGNOSIS:

- INR check in 3 days
- Daily weight monitoring
- Restrict salt and fluid intake

SAMPLE 3 — Acute Ischemic Stroke (MEDIUM RISK)
================================================================================

DISCHARGE SUMMARY

Patient Name: [REDACTED]
Date of Admission: 2026-03-22
Date of Discharge: 2026-03-29
Attending Physician: Dr. R. Menon, MD

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Sudden onset right-sided weakness and slurred speech.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
A 61-year-old male smoker with hypertension and hyperlipidemia
presented with sudden onset right hemiplegia, dysarthria, and facial
droop lasting more than 24 hours. NIHSS score at admission: 14.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:
- Hypertension (on treatment)
- Hyperlipidemia
- Smoking (40 pack-years)
- No prior stroke or TIA

MEDICATIONS ON ADMISSION:
- Amlodipine 10 mg once daily
- Atorvastatin 40 mg at night
- No antiplatelet therapy

INVESTIGATIONS:
- CT Brain: Left MCA territory infarct
- MRI Brain: Acute ischemic stroke left hemisphere
- ECG: Normal sinus rhythm
- Echocardiogram: No cardioembolic source
- HbA1c: 6.1%
- LDL: 4.2 mmol/L (elevated)
- Carotid Doppler: 60% left internal carotid stenosis

DIAGNOSIS:
1. Acute ischemic stroke (left MCA territory)
2. Left internal carotid artery stenosis (60%)
3. Hypertension
4. Hyperlipidemia
5. Active smoker

TREATMENT:
- IV Alteplase thrombolysis (within 4.5 hours)
- Aspirin 300 mg loading, then 75 mg daily
- Clopidogrel 75 mg once daily (dual antiplatelet for 21 days)
- Atorvastatin increased to 80 mg at night
- BP control: Amlodipine 10 mg continued
- Physiotherapy and speech therapy initiated

DISCHARGE MEDICATIONS:
- Aspirin 75 mg once daily
- Clopidogrel 75 mg once daily
- Atorvastatin 80 mg at night
- Amlodipine 10 mg once daily
- Smoking cessation counseling

FOLLOW-UP:
- Neurology review in 2 weeks
- Repeat carotid Doppler in 1 month
- Consider carotid endarterectomy
- Physiotherapy 3x/week

DISCHARGE CONDITION: Partial recovery, ongoing rehabilitation

DISCHARGE SUMMARY

Patient Name: [REDACTED]
Date of Admission: 2026-02-18
Date of Discharge: 2026-02-27
Attending Physician: Dr. V. Nair, MD

CHIEF COMPLAINT:
High fever, chills, and reduced urine output for 3 days.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
A 58-year-old male with end-stage renal disease on hemodialysis and
Type 2 diabetes mellitus presented with fever (39.8°C), rigors, hypotension
(BP 85/50), and confusion. Blood cultures were drawn immediately.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:
- End-Stage Renal Disease (on hemodialysis 3x/week)
- Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (insulin-dependent)
- Hypertension
- Peripheral vascular disease
- Multiple prior hospitalizations for sepsis

MEDICATIONS ON ADMISSION:

- Insulin lispro sliding scale
- Amlodipine 5 mg once daily
- Erythropoietin injections (thrice weekly)
- Aspirin 75 mg once daily
- Omeprazole 20 mg once daily

INVESTIGATIONS:
- WBC: 22.4 x10^9/L (severely elevated)
- CRP: 310 mg/L (critically elevated)
- Procalcitonin: 48 ng/mL (sepsis marker)
- Blood cultures: Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA)
- Creatinine: 9.8 mg/dL (ESRD baseline)
- Chest X-Ray: No consolidation, mild cardiomegaly
- Echocardiogram: No vegetations

DIAGNOSIS:
1. MRSA septicemia
2. End-Stage Renal Disease (on dialysis)
3. Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
4. Hypertension
5. Peripheral vascular disease

TREATMENT:
- IV Vancomycin (renally dosed, monitored by trough levels)
- IV Meropenem 500 mg every 24 hours (renal dose)
- Hemodialysis continued 3x/week
- IV fluid resuscitation (cautious — fluid overload risk)
- Insulin sliding scale for glycemic control
- ICU monitoring for first 48 hours

DISCHARGE MEDICATIONS:
- Oral Linezolid 600 mg twice daily for 14 days
- Insulin glargine 30 units at night
- Amlodipine 5 mg once daily
- Omeprazole 20 mg once daily
- Aspirin 75 mg once daily

FOLLOW-UP:
- Nephrology review in 3 days
- Repeat blood cultures in 1 week
- Continue hemodialysis schedule
- Infectious disease consultation
